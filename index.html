Executive Summary

Mission & Strategy

Positioning

"Aviation for the rest of us."

Private aviation is broken — not because the planes aren't there, but because the process is expensive, opaque, and built for insiders. Booking a charter today means cold-calling brokers, waiting for quotes, and paying markups you'll never see. GoFlight exists to change that.

We use AI to make booking a private flight as simple as sending a text. Faster quotes. Better prices. An experience that finally puts the customer first.

Transparency is non-negotiable. Every customer deserves to understand what they're paying for, what they're getting, and why. GoFlight's composable trip model breaks every charter into its real components — aircraft, fuel, crew positioning, deadhead, landing fees, FBO costs — so the price isn't a black box. If something changes, it's documented clearly and the customer sees exactly what shifted and why. No hidden markups. No mystery surcharges. Just an honest price for an honest flight.

Why Now

Three things have converged: AI has matured enough to power the entire charter workflow. Cloud infrastructure makes it possible to build and operate at a fraction of what it cost even two years ago. And the market is ready — customers are tired of the old model and operators and brokers want better tools and direct access to demand.

Go-to-Market

GoFlight serves the entire Part 135 ecosystem — not just operators and passengers, but brokers, FBOs, fuel providers, ground transport, catering, and every service provider that touches a charter trip.

  • Strategy: Operator-first — sign operators, build the supply side, then bring them customers
  • Launch market: US + EU. EBACE Geneva (June 2-4, 2026) is the international launchpad
  • Target market: Anyone in the Part 135 ecosystem — operators, brokers, FBOs, management companies, service providers
  • Target customer: Anyone who participates in Part 135 flights and accompanying services

The composable trip model is what makes this possible. Every trip is built from real components — aircraft, fuel, crew, ground services, FBO — which means service providers like American Flight Support, catering companies, and ground transport can all plug into the platform as part of the trip itself. GoFlight becomes the connective layer across the entire charter ecosystem.

The Platform

  • For customers: Just say where you're going. Wingman handles the rest.
  • For operators: Get booked directly. Transparent pricing. No middleman markup.
  • For service providers: Plug into trips as a component. Get discovered by operators and customers who need you.

Revenue Model

A $20/month subscription for all users keeps the platform serious and filters out "tire-kickers." GoFlight will also take a portion of the total trip price — starting at zero as the network builds, and growing as the platform proves its value to the ecosystem. The first pricing upgrade will be to 1.5%, then 3%.

Business Model

Revenue Streams

  • Subscription: $20/month per user — filters out tire-kickers and creates a recurring revenue base
  • Transaction fee: A portion of total trip price — starts at zero to build the network, grows as the platform proves its value to the ecosystem
  • Ecosystem services: As service providers (FBOs, fuel, ground transport, catering) plug into the composable trip model, each touchpoint becomes a potential revenue layer

Payment Flow

Customer pays → Escrow → Operator paid after delivery (24-72 hour hold). Full price transparency throughout — the customer sees what every component costs.

Payment Economics

Credit card processing fees (~2.5%) are embedded in the trip price, ensuring GoFlight's transaction fee (up to 3%) represents clean margin. Stripe powers all payment processing with built-in escrow protection.

Bank Transfer (ACH) — on the roadmap. Stripe supports ACH Direct Debit at 0.8%, capped at $5 per transaction. On a $50,000 charter, that's $5 in processing instead of ~$1,250 via credit card. ACH bank transfer will be offered as an alternative payment method for high-value bookings, dramatically improving unit economics for both GoFlight and its customers.

Go-to-Market

Phase 1 (Months 1–6): Industry events and direct outreach. EBACE Geneva, NBAA-BACE, and regional shows. Every event is a face-to-face onboarding opportunity with operators and brokers. At $20/month, the barrier to try GoFlight is nearly zero — less than a cup of coffee a day to access an AI-native platform that can save hours on every trip.

Phase 2 (Months 7–12): Network effects. Every operator who joins makes the platform more valuable for brokers, and vice versa. The Wingman's omnichannel reach — SMS, WhatsApp, email, voice — means operators can respond to quotes without changing their workflow. Private aviation is a tight-knit industry; word of mouth travels fast.

Phase 3 (Year 2–3): Ecosystem expansion. As service providers (FBOs, fuel, ground transport, catering) plug into the composable trip model, GoFlight becomes the connective layer for the entire trip. Each new integration is a distribution channel — the FBO on GoFlight recommends it to every operator they serve.

The economics: A traditional brokerage handling 200 customers typically requires 3-5 staff at $4,000-$6,000/month each ($12K-$30K/mo). GoFlight runs the entire platform for $462-$520/month depending on AI model choice — empowering brokers and operators to scale without scaling headcount. That's a 25-65x structural cost advantage. At 4,000 subscribers the math only gets better: serverless costs scale linearly while revenue scales exponentially with network effects.

Likely production approach: A hybrid model — fast/cheap models (GPT-4o mini or GLM Flash) for routing, status checks, and simple queries; premium models (Claude or GPT-4o) only for complex trip negotiations and high-value interactions.

Product

GoFlight is a full SaaS platform — responsive web app on mobile, tablet, and desktop — with every channel connected to a single AI called Wingman. Every participant in the charter ecosystem gets the interface they actually use, and every interaction flows through one composable trip model.

For Customers

Request a quote however you want — GoFlight meets you where you are.

  • Text or WhatsApp Wingman with where you're going and when — get back real quotes in seconds
  • Email a request to wingman@goflight.ai — Wingman builds the trip, reaches out to operators, and replies with options
  • Call Wingman by phone — voice-powered AI walks you through the booking conversationally
  • Use the web/mobile app for full trip management, payment, and tracking
  • Full price transparency — the composable trip model breaks every charter into real components so you see exactly what you're paying for and why

For Operators

Speed wins quotes. Operators can respond on any channel — seconds matter.

  • Real-time bid notifications via SMS, WhatsApp, email, or dashboard — respond to quotes instantly from any device
  • Operator dashboard for fleet management, bid history, analytics, and passenger flow
  • Competitive quoting — Wingman queries available aircraft across the network and returns price-sorted options to customers
  • Automated trip assembly — deadhead routing, fuel, crew positioning, landing fees computed in real time
  • Stripe payment processing with escrow protection (24-72 hour hold)

For Brokers

Wingman empowers brokers to handle 10x the volume without 10x the staff.

  • AI-powered quoting — Wingman does the legwork of sourcing, pricing, and assembling trips across the operator network
  • Customer management tools — track conversations, trips, preferences, and repeat booking patterns
  • Transparent pricing documentation — every component of the trip is itemized and traceable, building trust with customers

For Service Providers (FBOs, Fuel, Ground, Catering)

Wingman proactively reaches out to service providers on behalf of the operator or broker.

  • Plug into trips as a component — the composable trip model lets FBOs, fuel providers, ground transport, and catering appear as bookable services within any charter
  • Automated outreach from Wingman — when a trip needs ground transport or specific FBO services, Wingman contacts providers directly to coordinate and price
  • Get discovered by operators and customers who need your services in your region

Risks & Open Questions

Competitive Defense

Price and low overhead. GoFlight's entire operating infrastructure runs at $462–$520/month — a 25–65x cost advantage over competitors spending millions on legacy systems and large engineering teams. This means GoFlight can price aggressively, reach profitability faster, and survive longer without outside capital.

Speed to innovation. GoFlight's Dev Factory development methodology — leveraging AI-assisted autonomous development — shipped 19 features overnight and built 158 Lambda functions, 35 CI/CD pipelines, and 1,475 tests with a solo technical founder. While a funded competitor spends months hiring a team and aligning on architecture, GoFlight is already shipping the next generation of features. The combination of near-zero infrastructure cost and extraordinary development velocity creates a compounding advantage that is difficult for larger, slower organizations to match.

Regulatory & Compliance

Private aviation operates under FAA Part 135 certification, TSA security requirements, and DOT oversight. GoFlight's founding team has direct experience operating as a charter broker, but the full regulatory landscape for a technology platform facilitating Part 135 bookings is an area that requires deeper expertise as the company scales.

Understanding the compliance obligations that apply to GoFlight as a platform — distinct from those that apply to the operators themselves — is an active area of research and a priority as we move toward broader market adoption. We view this as an opportunity for the right partner or advisor to add significant value.
